Coffee in Philadelphia

Who does it, how, and why?

Coffee in Philly, in my time here, has gone from really sparse offerings, to having some very interesting options available.

Here are some great coffee options in Philly:

  • Reanimator - Sort of boring in a way, and pricy and snobbish, but they make decent coffee.
  • Menagerie - This is just a small shop - they don't roast their own beans, but have a terrific program.
  • Cloud Coffee - Like Menagerie, they do not roast their own, but have an amazing program.
  • Blind Tiger Coffee
  • Ultimo - Cult favorite of 3rd wave coffee enthusiasts.
  • Elixr - Cool shop, almost too cool.
  • Peddler - I have actually never been, but hear great things about this small shop.
  • Ox Coffee - Cool shop, not my favorite roasting style (overdeveloped), but good quality and good people.
  • Passenger - in the Bok building. I have not been here but what a cool looking place, and owner operated.
  • Shot Tower - cool little shop in Old City.

Now, here are a few shops that will make you cry, or regret your purchase, if you go there, but everyone seems to love anyway, for a number of reasons that I don't understand:

  • La Colombe - people love this place, but look the owner up, read about his maniacal plan, then consider the freakishly over-embellished shops they run and over-the-top product design, then cry a little bit.
  • Saxby's - go here if you enjoy:
    • Old milk
    • The scent of hockey gloves
    • Coffee from an industrial roaster
    • Sticky residue
    • Humidity
    • Thawed Milkshakes
  • Starbucks - everyone has them, philly loves them(????)
  • Dunkin (formerly Dunkin Donuts) - Yes, they fancy themselves a coffee operation
  • WaWa - any place that sounds like a baby named it, and they serve coffee and food, stay away
  • Ox Coffee - notice how it's also in the good list? They just over roast.
